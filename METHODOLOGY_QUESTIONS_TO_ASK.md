# Questions to Ask (Methodology Chapter – Section 3.8)

Use these when speaking to the developer. Write down everything they say so you can later form paragraphs for the dissertation.

---

## 1. Pre-implementation
- Did you create any drafts, wireframes, or sketches before coding?
- Did you build any prototypes (e.g. mockup, proof-of-concept) before full implementation?
- What planning or design work did you do before starting to code?

---

## 2. Testing during development
- Did you test during development or only at the end?
- Did you finish all the code before testing, or did you test in the middle?
- When did you test? (after each feature, at milestones, only at the end?)

---

## 3. Testing approach
- How did you test the system? (describe the process)
- How many times did you test? (approximate number of sessions or runs)
- What scenarios did you run? (e.g. clicking on the phishing page, entering credentials, checking the dashboard)

---

## 4. Types of testing
- Check your presentation: which testing types did you mention?
  - Black box testing
  - Unit testing
  - Integration testing
  - Functional testing
  - Usability testing
  - Other?
- For each type you used: what did you actually do? What did you test?

---

## 5. Methodology during implementation
- Did you have a methodology in mind whilst coding?
- How did your development process guide the way you built the system?
- Did you follow phases or stages, or was it more ad hoc?

---

## Tips
- Record or take detailed notes
- Note exact phrases they use (you can refine later)
- Ask for numbers (how many tests, how many iterations)
- After the conversation, paste your notes into AI and ask it to turn them into academic paragraphs for Section 3.8
