COMMENT FIX 1 – Clarify why silence is a gap worth filling
-----------------------------------------------------------

REVISED PARAGRAPH (combined with opening):

This dissertation started with a simple question: can watching how users behave on a phishing-style page tell us something that static tools miss? URL checks, certificates, and content similarity all run before or at page load. Once a user is on the page—clicking, typing, and scrolling—most anti-phishing tools go quiet. That silence matters because phishing often depends on user actions: entering credentials, following links, or responding to prompts. Static analysis cannot see those behaviours, yet how users interact (e.g., how quickly they act or how little they scroll before typing) can signal risk. PhishShield AI was built to explore whether that gap could be filled by behavioural monitoring. This chapter reflects on what that exploration achieved, what it means, and where it could go next.
